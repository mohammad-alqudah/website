Tourist guiding laws

Article 1

This system is called (the Tourist Guidance System of 1998) and it shall be enforced from the date of its publication in the Official Gazette.

Article 2

The following words wherever mentioned in this system shall have the meanings assigned to them below, unless the context indicates otherwise:
The Ministry: The Ministry of Tourism and Antiquities.
The Minister: The Minister of Tourism and Antiquities.
Committee: The Tourism Committee stipulated in the Tourism Law.
Tourist guide: The person who practices the work of guiding tourists and accompanying them to various tourist and archeological places in the Kingdom and providing them with information about them.

Article 3

a . The applicant for a license to practice the evidence profession must be:
1. Jordanian nationality, and he completed twenty years of age.
2. Not convicted of a felony or misdemeanor involving moral turpitude and public morals.
3. Holds a first university degree or equivalent, or a diploma in tourist guide services from an accredited college as a minimum.
4. Passed the English language test, which he mastered with knowledge of tourist information.
5. He passed the exam organized by the Ministry.
6. Full-time to practice the profession of evidence, and he may not practice any other job or profession, including trade and brokerage.
B. Upon the recommendation of the committee, the Minister shall grant the applicant a permit for a temporary period during which he shall attend the session held by the Ministry in coordination with the concerned authorities and pass the prescribed exams.
C. The Minister, upon the recommendation of the committee, may exclude any person from the provisions of Clause (3) of Paragraph (A) of this Article.

Article 4

The license granted to the tourist guide is divided into the following two categories:
a . A general guide that includes his work all over the Kingdom.
B. A site guide whose work is limited to a specific archeological site.

Article 6

a . The Ministry collects the following annual fees and allowances:
1. (40) dinars, a fee for a license to the General Tourist Guide.
2. (20) dinars, the site guide license fee.
3. (5) Dinars instead of a change of license information, or a lost or damaged one.
B. The part of the year is considered a full year for the purpose of collecting the annual fee when licensing for the first time.

Article 7

a . The application for renewing the license shall be submitted during the month of January of each year and everyone who fails to renew the license within this period shall pay an additional amount of (50%) of the annual license fee and if he does not renew the license until the end of February of that year he is prevented from practicing the profession to When the license is renewed within six months, the license is deemed null and void if it is not renewed during that article.
B. The license of the directory is not renewed if it becomes apparent to the committee that it has lost any of the license conditions stipulated in this system.

Article 8

a . A register for the names of practicing evidence shall be organized in the Ministry, and another record for the names of non-practicing statements.
B. The name of the evidence that is prohibited from practicing the profession shall be raised in accordance with the provisions of the tourism law in force by a decision of the Minister until the barrier is removed.
C. The evidence may, upon a justified request submitted to the Minister, raise his name from the practicing evidence record to the non-practicing evidence record, provided that this period does not exceed two years, and he has to pay the fees prescribed under this system if he requests to re-register it, and he must successfully pass the courses and exams prescribed for that.

Article 9

The Minister, upon the recommendation of the Tourism Committee, may fix the guide fees.

Article 10

a . The guide should not fail to perform his duties or services, and should not violate his obligations towards tourists or other tourist professionals, and he must in particular adhere to the following:
1. Show morals and honest citizenship to show Jordan in a good way.
2. Wear the scheduled outfit.
3. Putting his license on his chest during work and making it accessible to tourists and the Ministry's employees appointed for the purposes of oversight and any other employee authorized by the Minister in writing.
4. Submit his license to the Ministry when it is revoked or discontinued.
5. Carry out the tasks entrusted to him while accompanying the tourist groups, by explaining and giving adequate and accurate information and abiding by the specific dates of the tourism program.
B. The guide is prohibited from the following actions:
1. Exploiting his profession for immoral or commercial purposes and advertising himself in a way that violates the truth or involves deceiving visitors and tourists.
2. Accompanying both the tourist and the visitor in the archeological places, except at his request.
3. Do the indication while driving the car.
4. Requiring a commission or bonus request from the owners of tourism, commercial and other professions.
5. Requesting tips from tourist groups and visitors.
6. Drinking alcoholic drinks while working.
C. The Minister or whoever he authorizes in writing may conduct monitoring and inspection of the performance of the evidence to ensure the extent of his commitment to the provisions of this article.

Article 11

Whenever the need arises in accordance with the instructions issued by the Minister for this purpose, the evidence is subject to:
a . Tourist courses are held to ensure the extent of familiarity with tourist, archaeological and general information.
B. Exams to ensure that the guide maintains the language he is fluent in, or if he requests to be transferred from one site to another, or from one category to another, or to add a new language.

Article 12

a . The guide is exempt from paying entrance fees to tourist sites.
B. Tourism and travel offices must use a tourist guide to accompany tourist groups in the Kingdom and conclude a contract with the guide for each individual action in which the two parties' obligations are detailed.

Article 13

Whoever violates the provisions of this Bylaw or the instructions issued pursuant thereto, shall be punished with the fines stipulated in the tourism law in force.

Article 14

The Minister, upon the recommendation of the committee, may issue the necessary instructions to implement the provisions of this system, including the following:
a . Determining the university majors required to license the practice of the profession.
B. The foundations of foreign language exams, tourist and archaeological information, and course exams organized by the Ministry to give.
C. Organizing guide works and monitoring and inspection procedures.
